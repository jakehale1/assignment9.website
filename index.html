Work

I have gained diverse work experience across various industries, from agriculture to non-profit organizations. As an Undergraduate Researcher at the Lohmann Lab at the University of North Carolina Chapel Hill, I study the use of the Earth's magnetic field by sea turtles, salmon, and lobsters for long-distance navigation. My responsibilities include cultivating, breeding, and growing turtles, lobsters, and flounder, using the BORIS program to collect and analyze biological data, and analyzing trends between aquatic organisms and their environment.

I also gained valuable experience in social impact work as an Intern at No Kid Hungry in Bakersfield, CA. In this role, I was responsible for lunch and dinner distribution for 2000 children per day across 18 local sites, and supervised 5 high school interns. I also monitored food distribution, ensuring that meals were delivered to the correct recipients and FDA guidelines were followed, and created a three-week after-school teaching curriculum.

In addition, I have experience in retail as a Sales Clerk/Courtesy Clerk at Albertsons Grocery Store in Bakersfield, CA, where I provided customer service, worked the front-end checkout stand, and stocked shelves. I also worked as a Grape Picker at Cattani Farms, participating in the inspection, grading, sorting, storage, and post-harvest treatment of crops, and loading agricultural products into trucks.

For more details on my work experience, please see my resume.
